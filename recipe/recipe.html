Pad Kra Pao

    Ingredients:

  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 2 shallots
  • 2 red chilies
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 bunch of holy basil
  • 400g minced pork
  • fish sauce
  • light soy sauce
  • Kecap Manis (dark sweet soy sauce)
  • oyster sauce
  • neutral vegetable oil
  • 140g jasmin rice

Preperation:

  1. First place the jasmine rice in a sieve and wash the rice with cold water using your hands in circular movements. The excess starch causes the water to become cloudy. Wash the rice until the water runs clear.
  2. Then put the rice with 180 ml of water in the inner pot of the rice cooker . Close the lid and turn on the rice cooker. Once the light goes out, the rice is ready.
  3. Peel the garlic and shallots and chop both finely. Wash the chili peppers, remove the seeds and also chop finely. Crush the garlic, onions and chili in a mortar to form a seasoning paste. Wash the holy basil, shake it dry and pick off the leaves.
  4. Now for the Pad Kra Pao, heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a wok and add the seasoning paste. Fry the Pad Kra Pao paste over medium heat while stirring until it smells wonderfully aromatic. Now add the minced meat and fry on all sides. When frying, crumble the meat with a wooden spoon. When the meat is cooked, add 2 tbsp fish sauce, 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tbsp kecap manis and 1 tbsp oyster sauce and mix everything well.
  5. At the same time, heat some oil in a second pan and fry 2 fried eggs. You can fry the eggs normally, like we do. Or you can fry the eggs Thai-style, like in this video .
  6. Last but not least, add the holy basil to the meat. Now heat the Pad Kra Pao briefly again, then the Bai Krapao leaves will fall apart. But they must not turn black.
  7. And the Pad Krapao is ready. Simply arrange some rice and minced meat nicely on a plate and add a fried egg to each. In Thailand, this is served with nam pla prik, a mixture of fish sauce, lime juice and chili.
